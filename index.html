Why the equipment matters

Our automatic linear sharpening machine uses an electromagnetic chuck — the blade is held completely flat by magnetic force across its full surface, with zero clamping and zero deflection. Continuous coolant prevents heat buildup that would soften the steel. Every blade leaves at ±0.5° of factory specification, with set heights matched within 0.005"–0.010". That's drum balance, not just sharpness.

A dull blade costs more than a sharp one.

A blunt chipper blade doesn't just cut slower — it forces the engine harder, increases fuel burn, raises the risk of kickback, and puts stress on bearings that weren't designed to compensate for it. Most operators notice the symptoms (slower throughput, more noise, harder feeding) but absorb the cost without connecting it to blade condition.

$120 to sharpen a set of blades. One job pays for it. The alternative is running a machine that's working against itself — and eventually, a chipper that needs more than blades replaced.
