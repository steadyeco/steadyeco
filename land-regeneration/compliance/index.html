Licences

Licensed for the Work

01

Queensland Pesticide Licence

Licensed chemical applicator under Queensland pesticide legislation. Covers herbicide application for weed control operations across FNQ. Licence details available on request.

02

Chainsaw & Machinery Competency

Certified operation of chainsaws, mulching equipment, brushcutters, and chipper machinery. All equipment operated in accordance with QLD WHS regulations.

03

First Aid Certification

Current first aid certification for remote site operations. All on-site personnel maintain current first aid and CPR certification.

Work Health & Safety

Safe Work Practices

Safe Work Method Statements (SWMS)

Site-specific SWMS prepared for all government contracts and high-risk work. SWMS available for review prior to site commencement. Templates aligned to QLD Work Health and Safety Act 2011.

PPE & Site Safety

All personnel use appropriate PPE for ecological restoration work: helmets, hearing protection, eye protection, steel-capped boots, chainsaw chaps, and high-visibility gear as required.

Remote Site Protocols

FNQ remote site protocols in place for Cape York and outer Tablelands operations: communication plans, emergency procedures, and vehicle safety checks.

Equipment Maintenance

All machinery maintained to manufacturer specifications. Service records available for major equipment. Pre-start checks completed daily on active sites.

Incident Reporting

All incidents and near-misses recorded and reported in accordance with QLD WHS legislation. No lost-time injuries on record.

Environmental

Operating Within the Law

01

Environmental Protection Act (QLD)

All operations comply with the Environmental Protection Act 1994 (QLD). Chemical use, waste disposal, and waterway management practices meet or exceed EPA requirements.

02

Biosecurity Act (QLD)

Weed control operations comply with the Biosecurity Act 2014 (QLD). Council and landowner biosecurity obligations understood and met during all weed management programs.

03

Reef Protection Regulations

Erosion control and waterway restoration work designed to meet Queensland Reef Protection Regulations. Sediment and nutrient runoff minimisation built into all waterway works.

ERF & ACCU

Carbon Farming Credentials

Steady Eco Land Regeneration is developing capability across the two primary ACCU methodologies applicable to FNQ: Human-Induced Regeneration (HIR) and Environmental Planting.

Project development and ERF registration is conducted in partnership with a registered carbon project developer. MERI plans, monitoring protocols, and reporting obligations are embedded in all ACCU project structures.

ACCU Project Details

Primary Methodologies HIR, Environmental Planting
Registration Body Clean Energy Regulator (CER)
MERI Plans Included in all ACCU projects
Project Development In partnership with registered developer
Eligible Regions FNQ — Tablelands, Wet Tropics, Cape York
