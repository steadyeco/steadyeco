What We Do

Weed Control
Services Include:

FNQ's tropical climate makes invasive weed species one of the greatest threats to native ecosystems. Effective control requires local knowledge, the right methods for each species, and a commitment to follow-through — not a single treatment, but a managed program.

  • ✓ Mechanical weed removal and slashing
  • ✓ Targeted chemical weed treatment (licensed)
  • ✓ Integrated weed management programs
  • ✓ Invasive species mapping and reporting
  • ✓ Pond weed and aquatic vegetation management
  • ✓ Bush regeneration to SERA national standards
  • ✓ Post-clearing weed suppression and monitoring
  • ✓ Council reserve and public land weed programs
  • ✓ Riparian (waterway) weed control
Our Methods

Integrated Weed Management

Mechanical

Physical removal using chippers, slashers, and hand tools. Best for large-scale clearing, roadside management, and species where chemical methods are restricted (near waterways, organic properties, sensitive areas).

Chemical

Targeted herbicide application by licensed operators. Species-specific treatment protocols. Full compliance with Queensland chemical application regulations and safety requirements. Always documented.

Integrated

The most effective long-term approach — combining mechanical and chemical methods in sequence, followed by revegetation to prevent reinvasion. Recommended for council contracts and large properties.

Who This Is For

FNQ Councils

Public reserve management, roadside weed programs, waterway weed control, and annual maintenance contracts. We are procurement-registered and compliance-ready.

Landholders

Rural and pastoral properties managing invasive grasses, vines, and shrubs threatening productive land and native vegetation. We can treat and monitor across large areas.

Developers

Pre-construction weed clearing and post-project revegetation site preparation. Often required as a condition of environmental approvals in FNQ.
