What Are ACCUs?

Australian Carbon Credit Units —
Plain English

An ACCU (Australian Carbon Credit Unit) is a certificate that represents one tonne of carbon dioxide stored in or removed from the atmosphere. When you allow native vegetation to regrow on previously cleared land, that vegetation absorbs CO₂ as it grows.

The Australian government's Clean Energy Regulator issues one ACCU for every tonne of CO₂ stored. You can sell these credits to Australian companies who are required to offset their emissions.

At current prices, a well-vegetated property can generate meaningful annual income for up to 25 years — from doing nothing more than letting the bush grow back.

Step 01

Eligibility Check

We assess your land. Most cleared or degraded FNQ land with a history of native vegetation qualifies. Location, area, and clearing history all matter.

Step 02

CER Registration

We assist with registration under a Clean Energy Regulator (CER) approved method. The project runs for 25 years from registration.

Step 03

We Restore & Document

We plant, manage, and provide full documentation and monitoring to CER standards — the reporting requirement that proves credits are being earned.

Step 04

Credits Issued & Sold

ACCUs are issued to you at audit. You sell through the government spot price or directly to corporate buyers. We help you understand your options.

Landholder Income

Your Land Is an Asset

FNQ landholders with cleared grazing or agricultural land are sitting on a revenue opportunity that most haven't been told about. The ACCU scheme turns ecological restoration into a 25-year income stream.

You don't need to stop farming. You set aside a degraded corner, a waterway buffer, or land that's already unproductive — and let it regenerate. We handle the planting, the management, and the paperwork. You receive the credits.

The larger the area and the better the regrowth, the more credits generated. A 100 hectare project could generate thousands of ACCUs annually at $15–$35 per tonne CO₂ equivalent.
